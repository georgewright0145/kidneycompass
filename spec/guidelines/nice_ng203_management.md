# NICE NG203 — CKD Management (UK): BP targets, referral, anaemia, bone, ultrasound

> **Source & status.** Bootstrapped verbatim from the KidneyCompass PRD appendix, citing NICE NG203
> (2021, updated Nov 2024) and NICE TA775/TA942. **Pending UK renal-clinician sign-off.**
> Version: `nice-ng203-2024` / 2026-07-06. Verify against nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203.

## Blood-pressure targets (NICE, **clinic** measurements)
| Condition | Target |
|---|---|
| ACR **below 70 mg/mmol** | aim **below 140/90** mmHg |
| ACR **70 mg/mmol or more** | aim **below 130/80** mmHg |

Caveat to surface: home and clinic readings differ; these targets are **clinic-based**. Home BP
trends are context, not a substitute for clinic measurement.

## Referral criteria (NICE) — flag for discussion with GP; clinician makes the referral
Refer / discuss secondary-care referral when **any** of:
- 5-year **KFRE risk over 5%**
- **eGFR below 30** ml/min/1.73m²
- **ACR 70 mg/mmol or more**
- **A3 with haematuria**
- **Sustained or rapid fall in eGFR** (accelerated progression)
- Hypertension **uncontrolled on four or more** antihypertensive agents
- Suspected **renal artery stenosis**
- Suspected **rare or genetic** causes

## First-line drug management (generic, inform-and-route only)
- **ACE inhibitor or ARB** to maximum tolerated dose (foundational).
- **Statins** for cardiovascular risk.
- **SGLT2 inhibitors** per KDIGO 2024 / NICE TA775 (dapagliflozin) / TA942 (empagliflozin).
  KDIGO 2024 supports SGLT2i use across essentially the whole **eGFR 20–45** band regardless of
  albuminuria. Reassure on the **expected, non-alarming eGFR dip** on starting one.

## Anaemia (PRD §4.1, appendix)
- eGFR **under 60** prompts investigating whether anaemia is due to CKD.
- Consider treatment at **Hb ≤ 110 g/L** or symptoms → "raise with your GP" signal.
- Iron-deficiency markers: ferritin < 100 µg/L; transferrin saturation < 20%.

## Bone profile (later CKD)
- Measure **calcium, phosphate, PTH** in **G4/G5** (eGFR under 30).
- Treat vitamin D deficiency.
- Consider sodium bicarbonate if **eGFR < 30 and bicarbonate < 20 mmol/L**.

## Ultrasound (NICE indications)
Offer renal ultrasound for: accelerated progression; visible or persistent haematuria; symptoms of
urinary-tract obstruction; family history of ADPKD (with counselling); eGFR below 30; or if a biopsy
is being considered.

## Renal dose-review medicines (inform-and-route; PRD §4.6)
Medicines clinicians review by kidney function — flag for a medicines review, never instruct a change:
- **Metformin** — reviewed around eGFR 45; usually stopped below eGFR 30.
- **Nitrofurantoin** — generally avoided below eGFR 45.
- **Direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs)** — renally dose-adjusted.
- **Gabapentinoids** — accumulate in reduced kidney function.

**Sick-day rules (education only):** several medicines (ACEi/ARB, diuretics, SGLT2i, metformin,
NSAIDs — the "SADMANS" group) are often withheld temporarily during acute dehydrating illness and
restarted after recovery; this should be agreed with a clinician. Pair with "if acutely unwell or
unsure, contact your practice or NHS 111." Generic caution with OTC NSAIDs (nephrotoxins).
