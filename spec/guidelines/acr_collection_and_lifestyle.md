# ACR Collection (how to test correctly) + Lifestyle (UKKA)

> **Source & status.** Bootstrapped verbatim from the KidneyCompass PRD (§4.5, §4.7, appendix),
> citing NICE NG203 and the UK Kidney Association *Exercise and Lifestyle in CKD* (2021), Kidney
> Care UK. **Pending UK renal-clinician sign-off.** Version: `acr-lifestyle` / 2026-07-06.

## ACR — how to collect correctly (education; ACR is the early marker, often collected wrongly)
- **First-morning urine sample preferred.**
- **Avoid vigorous exercise in the 24 hours before.**
- **Do NOT collect during:** menstruation (a period), a urinary tract infection (UTI), a fever, or
  other acute illness — these **falsely raise** ACR.
- **A single raised ACR is not a diagnosis:**
  - New result **3–70 mg/mmol** → **confirm on a repeat first-morning sample.**
  - Result **≥ 70 mg/mmol** → does **not** need repeating.
- ACR **≥ 3 mg/mmol** is clinically important proteinuria.
- Framing: help the person get a **reliable test through their GP**, not a home diagnostic.

## Lifestyle (UKKA *Exercise and Lifestyle in CKD*) — generic, evidence-honest
- **Physical activity** (adapted from UK Chief Medical Officers' guidance, for an often older/frailer
  group): some activity is better than none; build toward **150 minutes of moderate activity a
  week**; strength, balance and flexibility work on **at least two days**; break up long sedentary
  periods. **Proven CKD benefits: lower BP, better physical function, better quality of life.**
  **Do NOT claim exercise directly slows eGFR decline** — the guideline finds that evidence mixed.
- **Smoking cessation** — the single strongest lifestyle lever for progression (**grade 1A**).
  Signpost to NHS cessation support.
- **Weight** — maintain a healthy weight (both under- and overweight raise risk; U-shaped).
- **Alcohol** — within national limits.
- **Diet** — individualised, professionally supervised advice on salt, potassium and phosphate by
  severity. **No low-protein diets.**

## Fluid guidance (deliberately cautious — PRD FAQ)
"Drink more water" is **wrong** for some people with CKD; in advanced disease or fluid overload the
advice flips to **restriction**. Fluid guidance stays **generic, trusted-source-based, and
escalates rather than personalising.**

## Case-finding testing risk factors (NICE; PRD §4.10)
Prompt an undiagnosed at-risk person to ask their GP for an **eGFR and a urine ACR** if they report:
diabetes, hypertension, cardiovascular disease, prior AKI, family history, or relevant long-term
medicines. This routes toward the test and the clinician; it is **not** a diagnosis.
