# Renal medication review + sick-day rules (reference for reviewing-medications)

Canonical source: `spec/guidelines/nice_ng203_management.md`. Inform-and-route only. Pending
UK renal-clinician sign-off.

## Dose-review by kidney function (flag for a medicines review; never instruct a change)
- **Metformin** — reviewed around eGFR 45; usually stopped below eGFR 30.
- **Nitrofurantoin** — generally avoided below eGFR 45.
- **DOACs** (apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban, dabigatran) — renally dose-adjusted.
- **Gabapentinoids** (gabapentin, pregabalin) — accumulate in reduced kidney function.

## Sick-day rules (education only)
SADMANS group — ACE inhibitors/ARBs, diuretics, SGLT2 inhibitors, metformin, NSAIDs — are often
withheld TEMPORARILY during acute dehydrating illness (vomiting, diarrhoea, fevers) and restarted
after recovery. Agree any change with a clinician. If acutely unwell or unsure → practice or NHS 111.

## Additional-medication prompt (generic)
SGLT2 inhibitors (dapagliflozin TA775, empagliflozin TA942): KDIGO 2024 supports use across
essentially the whole **eGFR 20–45** band regardless of albuminuria. Say generically that it is
usually considered and route to the GP; reassure on the expected, non-alarming eGFR dip on starting.

## OTC caution
Be careful with over-the-counter NSAIDs (nephrotoxins) — check with a pharmacist.
