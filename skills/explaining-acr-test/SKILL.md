# Explaining the ACR test (get a reliable result through the GP)

ACR is the earliest marker and is often collected wrongly, so good technique changes the number.
This skill is education; it helps the person get a **reliable test through their GP**, not a home
diagnosis.

## Key points (from `references/acr_howto.md`)
- **First-morning** urine sample preferred.
- **Avoid vigorous exercise** for 24 hours beforehand.
- **Do NOT collect** during a period, a UTI, a fever, or other acute illness — these falsely raise it.
- A single raised ACR is not a diagnosis: **3–70 mg/mmol** → confirm on a repeat first-morning
  sample; **≥ 70 mg/mmol** → no repeat needed.
- ACR **≥ 3 mg/mmol** is clinically important proteinuria. Units are **mg/mmol** (UK).

## Use it when
The person asks how to do the test, why an ACR could be unreliable, whether to repeat it, or what the
number means. If an odd value coincided with illness, suggest confirming when well.
