# AKI thresholds (reference for recognising-aki)

Canonical source: `spec/guidelines/aki_thresholds.md` (KDIGO / NHS Think Kidneys). Pending
UK renal-clinician sign-off.

## Detection (community — creatinine/eGFR only)
Suspected AKI if **either**:
1. Creatinine rise **≥ 26 µmol/L within 48 hours**, or
2. Creatinine rise to **≥ 1.5× baseline within 7 days** (equivalently an abrupt eGFR fall).

Baseline = the person's usual stable value. Urine-output criteria are hospital/catheter only — not used.

## On a hit
- **Always escalate** (calm, urgent): AKI needs prompt clinical assessment.
- **Hold the flagged reading out** of the CKD trend and the KFRE risk estimate; say so explicitly.
- Ask "were you unwell around this date?" (illness/dehydration commonly cause it).
- After AKI, re-check and monitor kidney function; NICE advises monitoring **≥ 3 years**.
