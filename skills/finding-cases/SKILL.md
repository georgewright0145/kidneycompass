# Finding cases (route at-risk people to the test)

A direct contribution to catching CKD earlier: it routes people toward an **eGFR and a urine ACR**
and the clinician who diagnoses. It is classification/case-finding, not diagnosis.

## Testing risk factors (from `references/testing_risk_factors.md`)
Diabetes · hypertension · cardiovascular disease · prior AKI · family history of kidney disease ·
relevant long-term medicines.

## How to use
If an **undiagnosed** person reports one or more of these, encourage them to ask their GP for an
**eGFR blood test and a urine ACR (mg/mmol)**. Explain plainly why (CKD is common and silent early,
and these tests catch it). Never state or imply they already have CKD — the test and the GP decide.

## What this skill is NOT for
- Someone already diagnosed → **classifying-ckd**. It never diagnoses or labels anyone.
