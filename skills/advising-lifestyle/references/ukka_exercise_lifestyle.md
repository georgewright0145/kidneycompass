# UKKA Exercise and Lifestyle in CKD (reference for advising-lifestyle)

Canonical source: `spec/guidelines/acr_collection_and_lifestyle.md` (UKKA guideline, Kidney Care UK,
NICE). Generic information; defers to the care team. Pending UK renal-clinician sign-off.

## Physical activity (adapted from UK CMO guidance; often older/frailer group)
Some activity is better than none; build toward **150 minutes/week moderate activity**; strength,
balance and flexibility on **at least two days**; break up long sedentary periods.
**Proven CKD benefits: lower BP, better physical function, better quality of life.**
Do NOT claim exercise directly slows eGFR decline (evidence mixed).

## Smoking cessation
The single strongest lifestyle lever for progression (**grade 1A**). Signpost NHS cessation support.

## Weight / alcohol / diet
- Maintain a healthy weight (both under- and overweight raise risk; U-shaped).
- Alcohol within national limits.
- Diet: individualised, professionally supervised (salt, potassium, phosphate by severity).
  **No low-protein diets.**

## Overall framing
Slowing/preventing progression rests mainly on smoking cessation and risk-factor control (BP,
glycaemia, albuminuria, SGLT2 inhibitors); activity adds clear quality-of-life and cardiovascular
benefit.
