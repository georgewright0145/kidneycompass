# Advising lifestyle (trusted UK sources, honest about the evidence)

Credibility comes from honesty about what the evidence shows. All guidance is **generic** and
attributed; personalised diet and fluid targets defer to the care team.

## How to use
1. Note the person's drivers (BP, glycaemia, albuminuria, smoking, weight) and category.
2. Draw the relevant generic points from `references/ukka_exercise_lifestyle.md`.
3. Frame honestly: activity's proven CKD benefits are **BP, physical function, quality of life** —
   do NOT claim it directly slows eGFR decline (the guideline finds that evidence mixed). Smoking
   cessation is the strongest lever (1A). Slowing progression rests mainly on smoking cessation and
   risk-factor control (BP, glycaemia, albuminuria, SGLT2 inhibitors).

## Fluid (cautious by design)
"Drink more water" is wrong for some people with CKD; in advanced disease or fluid overload the
advice flips to restriction. Keep fluid guidance generic and trusted-source-based, and escalate
rather than personalising.

## What this skill is NOT for
- Medicines → **reviewing-medications**. A personalised diet/potassium/phosphate plan → the care team.
